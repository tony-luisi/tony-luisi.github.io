heading 1

What's your take on the DBC / EDA experience?

The EDA experience I think is focused on a group of people getting together who are enthuastic, committed and hungry about learning about software development and coding.

What are your impressions?

I think that the EDA culture is really attuned to people who really want to be there (in the words of Shareef - a creator mindset), and don't want to take the conventional path to programming (i.e. spending three years at a University). So far, I am really impressed and can't wait to get to the bootcamp.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

Because of where I am in my life (i.e. I have spent 8 years since graduating University involved in performing IT and System Audits for some of the biggest businesses in NZ), I am not keen on wasting my time. From what I know of the culture so far, I will be surrounded by people from all walks of life, who are hungry and eager to learn. I think being in this kind of environment will magnify my ability to learn and grow, and is something I have been looking for.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

Not really, however, I didn't expect so many people to actually be from overseas.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I am super excited and enthusiastic to be given this opportunity, however, yes it makes me very nervous.
